Deaf design researcher at the intersection of disability, AI & accessibility.

Nina Bernard‑Tran (she/her/they) is a first‑generation immigrant raised in Seattle, WA, and now based in Nova Scotia, Canada. She is a Deaf design researcher and instructional designer with 6+ years of experience spanning accessibility‑centered design, mixed‑methods research, usability testing, and UX/UI practice. Her work is grounded in advocacy for Deaf women of color in human–computer interaction (HCI) and is driven by a commitment to equity, access, and community‑centered approaches to technology design.

Nina brings a strong interdisciplinary background in instructional design, user experience and user interaction (UX/UI), and collaborative research across technical and social domains. She is particularly interested in the sociotechnical intersections of HCI, disability, and accessibility, with research interests that include accessible AI and wearables, sign language technologies, disability studies, and the design of inclusive and culturally responsive usability testing methods.
